Who Made Fallout: New Vegas?

Fallout: New Vegas is a critically acclaimed role-playing game developed by Obsidian Entertainmentand published by Bethesda Softworks. Released in 2010, the game is set in a post-apocalyptic world and is the fourth main installment in the Fallout series. In this article, we’ll delve into the history of the game’s development and explore the key players involved.

The Concept and Development

Fallout: New Vegas was conceived by Obsidian Entertainment, a studio founded in 2003 by a group of veteran game developers, including Feargus Urquhart,Chris Avellone and Chris Parker. The studio had previously worked on several notable games, including Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II and Neverwinter Nights 2. In 2008, Obsidian Entertainment began working on Fallout: New Vegas, with a team of around 100 developers. The game was initially intended to be a spin-off, rather than a full-fledged sequel to the previous Fallout games. The team drew inspiration from the original Fallout games, as well as other classic RPGs, such as Baldur’s Gate and Planescape: Torment.

Key Developers

Several key developers played a crucial role in the creation of Fallout: New Vegas. Some of the most notable include:

  • Feargus Urquhart: Co-founder of Obsidian Entertainment and the game’s project lead.
  • Chris Avellone: Co-founder of Obsidian Entertainment and the game’s lead designer.
  • Josh Sawyer: Lead designer and writer, responsible for creating the game’s story and characters.
  • J.E. Sawyer: Designer and writer, who worked closely with Josh Sawyer on the game’s story and characters.

Gameplay and Features

Fallout: New Vegas features a unique blend of exploration, combat, and role-playing elements. The game takes place in a post-apocalyptic world, set in the Mojave Desert and the city of New Vegas. Players take on the role of the Courier, a character who is tasked with delivering a package to the New Vegas Strip.

The game features a variety of gameplay mechanics, including:

  • Character Creation: Players can create their own character, choosing from a range of skills, perks, and abilities.
  • Combat: The game features turn-based combat, with a variety of weapons and abilities to use.
  • Exploration: Players can explore the game’s vast open world, completing quests and discovering hidden secrets.
  • Dialogue: The game features a complex dialogue system, allowing players to interact with non-playable characters (NPCs) in a variety of ways.

Reception and Legacy

Fallout: New Vegas received widespread critical acclaim upon its release, with many praising its engaging story, memorable characters, and improved gameplay mechanics. The game has since become a cult classic, with a dedicated fan base and a lasting impact on the RPG genre.

The impact of Fallout: New Vegas on modern game development

Even after 16 years, Fallout: New Vegas remains a benchmark of the genre. People still enjoy playing it today, with some replaying it to explore new, undiscovered possibilities, as well as using it as a source of inspiration when creating their own games. An example is The Outer Worlds series, which adopted the best aspects of the role-playing system and the game's immersive atmosphere.Cyberpunk 2077 also, although mostly futuristic, shows elements of choice, non-linearity, and a rich world inspired by New Vegas. In the future, Fallout: New Vegas will continue to serve as a model for some game developers and as a beloved game for replaying.
